While the vastness of the universe and the presence of numerous planets increase the probability of extraterrestrial life, this alone does not constitute concrete evidence that aliens are real. Probability is not proof; the absence of direct, verifiable contact or unequivocal scientific evidence means the claim remains speculative. The existence of extremophiles on Earth shows life can adapt to extreme environments here, but it doesn't guarantee similar life elsewhere. Unidentified aerial phenomena are simply unidentified—they do not automatically imply alien origin. Many sightings have been explained by natural or man-made causes. Until we have reproducible, rigorously verified evidence—such as biological samples, communication signals, or unambiguous artifacts—the assertion that aliens exist remains an unproven hypothesis. Relying on possibility rather than proof weakens scientific rigor, making opposition to the motion both reasonable and necessary.
